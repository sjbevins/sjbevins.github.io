About Me

I am a physics PhD Student at the College of William and Mary. My research focuses on Lattice Quantum Chromodynamics (Lattice QCD), with a particular emphasis on the calculation of the pion form factor and boosting the pion to higher momenta.

I earned my B.S. in both Pure Mathematics and Physics from Virginia Commonwealth University. I am passionate about using mathematics to tackle challenging problems in physics and am always eager to collaborate on exciting projects.

Beyond research, I enjoy reading and engaging in American politics. This website serves as a central hub for my academic work, projects, and occasional thoughts. Nothing here is endorced by any of my employers and all opinions are my own, even though everyone agrees of course.
